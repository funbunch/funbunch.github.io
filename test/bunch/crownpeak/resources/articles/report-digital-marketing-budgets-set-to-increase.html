Report: Digital marketing budgets set to increase

Organizations are prepared to increase their digital marketing budgets in 2013, with content marketing, search engine optimization (SEO) and mobile marketing among the top areas of growth, according to a new survey from Econsultancy. The firm's annual Marketing Budgets report found that 71 percent of organizations plan to increase their digital marketing budgets in the year ahead, while just 2 percent anticipate a decrease.

As digital marketing investments grow, content marketing is the discipline expected to see the greatest budgeting increases, with 70 percent of organizations saying they plan to expand their spending on the tactic in 2013. Additionally, 65 percent of organizations said they plan to increase spending on organic search optimization, 62 percent anticipated growth in budgets for social media engagement and 61 percent expect to devote more resources to mobile marketing for acquisition.

"Digital marketing technology continues to attract significant investment as marketers aim for cross-channel integration and optimization," Econsultancy researchers noted.

To handle multiple channels of engagement and optimization analytics data in an increasingly content-rich environment, organizations may need to evaluate the technology options such as content management systems (CMS) that are supporting their digital marketing efforts. Choosing the best enterprise CMS will require a consideration of factors such as analytics tracking, mobile solutions and integration with channels such as social media.

How increased investment will play out in marketing trends

As investment in digital marketing grows, the industry can expect to see a rise in digital agencies in 2013, according to a recent iMedia Connection column by marketing specialist Jennifer Wong. Additionally, partnerships between agencies and technology solutions will become important as organizations look to leverage advanced tools in their digital marketing efforts.

Wong noted that marketers will be expected to become "data geeks" to determine the effectiveness of different channels. Understanding ROI of various approaches as organizations branch into mobile, social media, email and content marketing. Marketers will also have to become comfortable with more complex back-end tools to manage different channels and better integrate data sources from various APIs, Wong suggested. Some understanding of back-end integration is likely to be a requirement for marketing professionals.

One of the areas in which Wong anticipated technology playing a central role is in leveraging marketing and application data to craft personalized web experiences. Web personalization is increasingly an expectation as organizations use technology to improve audience interaction.

"User experience is becoming a major part of every website, and personalization is at its core," Wong wrote. "Personalization will grow, and services that offer the ability to personalize experience will emerge as well - whether it is cookie-based, login-based, or self-select preferences."

Today's enterprise web CMS offerings will simplify many digital marketing goals in the year ahead, offering painless integration of various applications and data streams, as well as web personalization tools that can turn these sources of information into actionable features. As investment in various digital channels grows, organizations can expect to budget more for technology solutions designed to handle and make the most of those channels as well.
