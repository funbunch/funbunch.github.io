Integrate SEO and PPC data to achieve better search results

Marketers often debate about the effectiveness of search engine optimization (SEO) campaigns as opposed to pay-per-click (PPC) marketing, but these two practices are becoming increasingly converged - from marketers' and Google's point of view. Google is moving toward more organic paid search results, while organizations increasingly see SEO and PPC as practices that should be handled with the same data and staff. By thinking of the two as components of the same effort and using tools that facilitate integration of information, organizations can strengthen both SEO and PPC.

"This isn't a topic you can simply throw pointed remarks at, like 'SEO is way better than paid search!' or 'PPC is SO much more awesome than SEO,'" CEO Adam Audette of AudetteMedia wrote in a column for SearchEngineWatch. "Each is a unique, and complementary, online marketing channel."

Audette noted that paid search should not take anything away from organic search budgets, and, similarly, more limited analytics should not discourage marketers from tracking the return on investment (ROI) of SEO campaigns. He explained that Google is experimenting with new techniques to make PPC more attractive to organizations and to compete with the value of organic search.

Earlier this year, the search engine overhauled its AdWords policy to reflect the shift toward valuing quality content in organic results. Google tightened its policies to restrict sites that exist primarily to show ads and sites that are not transparent in their ads. Metrics such as landing page loading times, which already factor into SEO, are likely to play an increasingly large role in PPC rankings, Matt Issacs, a digital marketing CEO, told The Drum. As these types of changes make the PPC landscape more similar to that of SEO, marketers can expect the tactics used for each to converge and complement each other, according to several industry experts

How to combine SEO and PPC practices

The Drum recently asked a number of marketing experts how to use PPC to benefit SEO and vice versa. Many agreed that SEO can provide valuable information for discovering new long-tail keywords, while PPC data makes it easier to test new keywords and discover ideas for new content themes. Marketers will increasingly want to adopt tools that allow them to integrate this data to incorporate these insights across different channels.

Additionally, incorporating PPC practices may help SEO professionals increase their focus on the landing page experience. Rather than spending as much effort on landing high search rankings, SEO marketers could benefit from a PPC mindset that looks at the user journey through the site and conversion rates, several experts noted.

"Make it useful and get straight to the point," Ben Hatton, a managing director at a digital marketing agency, told The Drum. "PPC has long since focused on wringing everything out of a click you pay for with landing page testing and conversion tracking. SEO needs to take this to heart. Too often, SEO landing pages are focused on the googlebot - delivering crawlable content - when the user just wants to get in touch or to buy something."

Audette explained that PPC approaches can help SEO professionals think differently about the value of ranking for different terms. By applying more rigorous analytics to determine the importance of specific phrases, marketers can devote their efforts to terms that yield more conversions or better ROI in the same way they would shift a PPC budget to those terms.

He noted that the principal barrier to doing this in many organizations is that technology does not allow it. By implementing tools such as a cloud content management system (CMS) that facilitates integration of different analytics applications, marketers may be able centralize their data and better synchronize their PPC and SEO efforts.
