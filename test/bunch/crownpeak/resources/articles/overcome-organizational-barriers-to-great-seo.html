Overcome organizational barriers to great SEO

Keeping up with evolving SEO trends is inherently difficult given the rapid pace of change in the field, but maintaining best practices within a large organization can be particularly challenging due to bureaucratic roadblocks. Instituting a successful enterprise SEO campaign may require specific strategic considerations.

Forbes contributor Brent Gleeson and SearchEngineLand writer Ian Lurie both offered suggestions for handling common issues in recent columns.

Achieving organizational alignment

A lack of clear executive goals can make it difficult for marketers and IT staff to work together in establishing SEO as a priority, Lurie wrote. In many organizations, data is siloed, as are different marketing channels. If marketers cannot access web traffic data, or if content and social media teams are advancing different agendas, accomplishing SEO goals becomes significantly more challenging.

Organizations should develop a clear keyword governance strategy so that each team knows where its SEO priorities lie, Gleeson suggested. Additionally, by communicating about KPIs, different teams can ensure they are directing their audience to the right pages or digital assets, and internal groups can "promote each other instead of diluting focus by competing for similar or identical goals."

Adopting new practices

In a large organization, getting clearance to go ahead with a new idea or respond to changing strategy trends, such as those driven by a new search algorithm update, can be a long process, Lurie noted. Having a content strategy that responds with agility to the news cycle can be difficult when every piece of content needs executive clearance, for instance. Likewise, implementing new tactics amid a chorus of competing managerial pressures can require careful navigation.

"The larger the group, the greater the chance for chaos," Lurie wrote.

However, understanding changes in SEO best practices and responding to them is essential for ongoing success, Gleeson wrote. To get the kind of agile response necessary for success, Lurie recommended taking time to educate stakeholders throughout the organization and building relationships in advance. By developing trust before tough situations arise and advancing priorities a little at a time, SEO professionals can create a framework for success.

Implementing the right technology

A new SEO plan can be stopped before it even begins by slow development turnaround, Lurie wrote. Organizations hampered by an unwieldy, unscalable on-premise content management system (CMS) are likely putting an unnecessary burden on their IT departments and limiting their ability to respond to a changing search environment, Gleeson said.

"You must have an understanding of how your enterprise technology systems are going to play into your SEO strategy," he wrote, noting that every organization should ensure its CMS generates SEO-friendly URLs, translates content successfully for all regions and reflects localized keyword priorities.
