The Challenges

A CHANGE IN BUSINESS MODEL

In addition to its very popular Internet communication software product, Skype’s original business model included an e-commerce website component through which it sold Skype certified VOIP hardware directly to its end users.

Over time, Skype encountered difficulties in scaling this approach operationally, primarily due to the large amount of time, inventory management overhead, content changes and support costs it required to sell and deliver the hardware to each customer in each country. To expand to more countries and offer more products to its users at the best prices, Skype had to change its business model. As a result, Skype shifted to offering these products through a global network of third party merchants.

In Skype’s new business model, sales of all Skype Certified hardware worldwide, including hardware such as headsets and webcams and VOIP phones, would be linked from the Skype store to local online merchants, who would then complete the sale.
