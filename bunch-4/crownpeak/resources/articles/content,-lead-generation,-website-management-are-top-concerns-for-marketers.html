Content, lead generation, website management are top concerns for marketers

The most effective marketers are always willing to adopt new strategies and techniques to accomplish their goals. Recently, a survey from software firm Optify outlined some of the habits of the top marketers working today.

While the survey recorded a variety of approaches to marketing, some practices continually emerged among the top-performing respondents, including attention to planning and time spent creating content. Organizations that want to follow in the highly effective footsteps of these marketers can take note of the areas that receive the most focus and implement similar strategies.

"Star performers"

The survey identified certain respondents who were considered "star performers" based on their ability to consistently meet lead generation targets through their marketing initiatives. Of the 250 B2B marketers sampled for the report, 47 percent fell into this category.

Those who outperformed their peers allotted more time for planning their strategies, the report showed. While 73 percent of all respondents indicated they spend less than five hours per week planning, 42 percent of "star performers" spend more than five hours on this task, with 14 percent reporting they spend 15 hours or more.

In addition to planning, high-performing marketers spend a significant amount of time generating marketing content. In fact, that category occupied nearly one-third of the total amount of time spent executing a marketing plan. Lead generation (17 percent) also occupied a lot of time. Much of their lead generation and content marketing efforts were directed at social media channels, with 84 percent of respondents reporting that they spend time working with the platform each week.

Content-driven lead generation

When following the same path as these top marketers, organizations will need to exercise some best practices of their own to meet their lead generation targets. Recently, Business 2 Community highlighted some of the top considerations for creating business-to-business marketing content, particularly for social media channels.

One of the most important aspects of successful B2B content marketing is an authoritative, expert voice, the article said. Providing relevant analysis of emerging trends and changing market conditions is an excellent way for an organization to position itself as a leader in its field, which can build trust among potential client

Nevertheless, organizations shouldn't overlook an aspect of fun and entertainment in their social media content. Creative, personal content has a much better chance of standing out than flat material, the article said. Certain social media platforms like Pinterest also allow the use of pictures and other visuals, which can go a long way toward creating eye-catching content.

Additional voices

Another effective approach to content marketing is the guest blog, according to marketing expert Andy Crestodina. Writing on the MarketingProfs website, Crestodina noted that an effective guest blogger produces content that is rich with keywords and will drive traffic to an organization's site. In exchange, the organization links its website to the blogger's, creating an interconnected social web that continually drives traffic and generates leads.

Content management practices

Simply posting material to an organization's social media page or website is not enough to drive leads, according to Business 2 Community. Content must be constantly updated to keep it from becoming stale or useless to potential clients. One way to keep material fresh is by recycling it into new formats, the website said. For example, blog posts could be recorded as podcasts or surveys could be turned into infographics.

Web content management occupied roughly 16 percent of time spent engaging in marketing campaigns, according to marketers responding to the software firm's survey, and 45 percent said they outsource their content management to a third party. An effective content management system (CMS), however, could eliminate the need for outsourcing. However an organization decides to approach its marketing campaigns, some of these practices could help generate leads.
