Areas to improve B2B SEO in 2013

As B2B marketers evaluate search engine optimization (SEO) strategies for the year ahead, they may want to consider some emerging web enhancements that are likely to become central to B2B search engine marketing in the near future. A recent Search Engine Land column by marketer Derek Edmond highlighted some of these. In particular, Edmond suggested B2B marketers implement authorship tagging and use a tag management system.

Maximizing the value of authorship tagging

Google's authorship tool is designed to make high quality content easier for users to discover, and, as a result, it could represent an opportunity for B2B marketers to make great material stand out. Marketers who present their organization's web content in a form that takes advantage of authorship will be able to position the organization as a search engine and social media leader, Edmond suggested.

"A successful authorship implementation could positively impact the organizationâ€™s overall brand presence, establishing thought leadership organizationally and individually," he wrote

A separate Search Engine Land article reported that users who spend a certain period of time - longer than two minutes, for instance - on an article by a specific author and then return to search results will see more results from that author. This feature can therefore help organizations drive website traffic with influential content.

Employing a tag management system

Edmond also predicted that organizations will be able to improve the tracking of marketing campaigns in the year ahead by implementing tag management systems (TMS). A TMS works in concert with a content management system (CMS) to control and implement externally-hosted JavaScript page tags such as those used by Google Analytics. A CMS Wire article explained that marketers often go around IT staff to add JavaScript code to sites, which can create performance and control problems, in addition to creating a rift between these departments. By using a TMS, organizations can centralize control of such code and standardize its rollout in a way that satisfies both groups.

"The result is often dramatic time savings associated with tag-based system deployments," analytics consultant Eric T. Peterson wrote for CMS Wire.

By giving B2B marketers more control over tracking code, a TMS can improve the flexibility of marketing campaigns and reduce confusion between marketing and IT. A CMS that integrates smoothly with a TMS can offer organizations a way to take advantage of such tools.

Organizations that adopt new practices in B2B search engine marketing such as authorship and TMS implementation can position themselves for success in the year ahead.
