Understand the SEO benefits of social media

Marketers have embraced social media, recognizing its potential for drawing audiences and fostering interaction. According to eConsultancy, 97 percent of marketers agree that social media adds value to their organization as a tool for brand discovery and awareness or for customer engagement. A Content Marketing Institute survey found that 87 percent of marketers are using social media for content marketing purposes.

A recent eConsultancy article noted that, despite the rise in social media marketing, many organizations are still neglecting social media's SEO benefits. Social media is becoming a bigger part of the way people get their information - one-third of American adults under 30 use social media as their primary news source, according to the Pew Research Center - and, as a result, social signals are factoring into search engine algorithms. Econsultancy's Jennifer Dunphy advised marketers to ensure that their websites are sending the kinds of signals that Google's algorithms are increasingly rewarding.

“Large amounts of social shares indicate that a mass amount of people not only found what they were looking for on your site, but also liked your site enough to share it with others,” she explained. “It also shows the search engines and the sites visitors that your site is up to date and that your site invests in making the user experience a positive one.”

The importance of quality content

To draw social attention, organizations need to produce content that will draw audience attention and prompt viral sharing, Dunphy wrote. Web content, blog posts, videos, images, case studies, white papers, articles and how-to guides are all types of shareable content. Each of these should also be optimized for a social sharing context, as visitors who first encounter content over social media will interact with it differently than visitors who arrive on a site through organic search.

Since social traffic relies on real people to generate results, it may prompt more meaningful interactions. However, people will only share content with actual value and worthwhile messaging. Marketers will need to focus on creating material that people are willing to endorse, and those who do will see the results, Dunphy said.

“Viral content is like the mass endorsement of the public,” she wrote. “Every share, like, re-tweet, +1 , subscription, and pin means one more endorsement for your website, simultaneously increasing your social capital and your search creditability. By gaining a massive amount of social shares, you’re not just boosting your SEO signals and your site visibility - you’re also creating content with value for your customer base.”

The SEO goal of social media is to maximize the profile of this content for search engines. As a social media audience spreads quality content to new niches and markets, search engines will reward the organization's website.

The distribution of shareable content will also build a site's presence through links on social networks or other organizations' websites. A recent Forbes column from SEO expert Todd Malicoat noted that, even as social signals grow, site's link graph will remain important. By investing in social media efforts, organizations can strengthen both types of signals and position themselves for SEO success in a changing online environment.
