Satisfaction with cloud services could spark market explosion

Software-as-a-Service solutions for the enterprise are no longer an emerging trend. Various reports have indicated that cloud-based business tools are being used by a significant number of organizations, and the widespread adoption of these services is only expected to increase in the near future.

As satisfaction with cloud services improves and organizations find they are able to merge SaaS tools with existing infrastructures, cloud adoption will increase, becoming an indispensable part of day-to-day operations, regardless of industry.

Cloud coverage

Early adopters of cloud-based solutions were quick to tout their benefits, but fears over security have kept a number of organizations from implementing these tools. In a recent InformationWeek survey of business technology professionals across a variety of industries, 36 percent of organizations that are not using SaaS tools listed security as their main concern.

But security in the cloud has improved in recent years, and that wary segment of the business crowd is the exception rather than the rule. Nearly two out of five respondents to the InformationWeek survey said their organization uses SaaS solutions. And among those that use the cloud, overall satisfaction is higher than among those that use on-premise services, according to Lorna Garey, content director of InformationWeek Reports.

Far and away the most important consideration organizations make when deciding on an SaaS solution is its ability to integrate seamlessly with existing business infrastructures, with one-third ranking this their top priority.

Cloudy forecast

It's evident that a variety of business functions, including marketing, customer support and sales, will soon be operating in the cloud, said IT expert Dion Hinchcliffe in a recent article for ZDNet. In commenting on cloud adoption, Hinchcliffe said SaaS services for non-mission-critical services are essentially "a foregone conclusion.

He may be right. A recent market analysis from International Data Corporation found that the current market for cloud services is roughly $40 billion. The next four years, however, are a different story. IDC projects that the market for cloud services will catapult forward, growing at a compound rate of 26.4 percent per year to hit $100 billion by 2016.

"By the end of the decade, IDC expects at least 80 percent of the industry's growth, and enterprises' highest-value leverage of IT, will be driven by cloud services and the other 3rd Platform technologies," said Frank Gens, senior vice president and chief analyst at IDC.

Improved satisfaction with cloud services and a rapidly expanding market are likely to convince even the most stalwart SaaS skeptics that moving to the cloud is in their best interest.
