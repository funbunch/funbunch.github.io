Creating a guest blogging approach that adds value

Guest blogging has emerged as a popular technique for achieving marketing results that range from tapping into a new audience to improving search engine optimization (SEO). Not all guest blogging approaches are created equal, however. While using an authoritative guest blogger can be a great way for an organization to tap into expert insights and generate high-quality content, some sites utilize undistinguished guest bloggers as just another way of churning out links to help with search engine ranking. However, this approach is likely to hurt a site and the brand associated with it.

A flood of low-quality content

A recent video from Google explored the value of guest blog posts and prompted discussion of the best way to leverage guest bloggers.

Google is trying to minimize the practice of websites fishing for backlinks to boost SEO rankings by accepting a high volume of guest blogger content that is poorly written, according to SiteProNews columnist Donna Anderson’s analysis of a video by Google spokesman Matt Cutts. Anderson stressed that publishing low quality guest blog posts might not only hurt sites’ page rankings but also their credibility with readers.

Anderson pointed out that readers likely come to a high-profile blog because they respect the quality and authority of the blogger, and allowing lower-quality content on the blog can compromise the blog's authority. Blogs that accept too much guest content risk turning into article directories that offer little return value, she explained.

Any blog worth actually being on has tuned out SEOs,” wrote Search Engine Watch’s David Harry, explaining his frustration with SEO firms bombarding blog owners with requests to place guest posts.

How to effectively use guest blogging

While Google's SEO algorithms may begin punishing sites that include too much low-quality guest posting regardless of how many links the posts generate or how highly their keywords rank, the value of a quality guest blog post may be greater than ever as well. On an increasingly social web, expert personalities can stand out and add real value and visibility to a brand’s web presence. Anderson suggested reaching out to the type of high-quality content creators that Google values in its algorithm.

“Which bloggers do you look up to?” Anderson wrote. “Who do you turn to as your own source of information? These are the guest bloggers you want. These are the guest bloggers who will add value to your blog. These are the guest posts that will survive the updates if Google decides to start penalizing blog owners for posting poor-quality guest blog posts.”

Search Engine Journal contributor an Ripoll recently pointed out that leveraging social media is important for making these guest posts part of a wider conversation. Quality guest bloggers are likely to be familiar to a sizeable audience, and may also be actively driving conversation and action on social media, Ripoll said. Engaging these guest bloggers as contributors can help a website become part of that larger social media dialogue.

When used effectively, guest bloggers should add expert insight and drive social media engagement with a brand, and so should not just be seen as a way of populating an organization’s site with more content.
