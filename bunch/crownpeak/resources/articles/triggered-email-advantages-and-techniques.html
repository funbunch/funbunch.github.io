Triggered email advantages and techniques

With a variety of approaches available for email marketing, organizations need to make the best use of their contact lists to generate leads and convert sales. Collecting user data, creating segmented lists and directing timely messages to users based on their online and social media habits is a proven, effective approach to embarking on an email marketing campaign.

Triggered email

A recent report from the Direct Marketing Association's Email Experience Council indicated that nearly three out of five new subscribers on an average email list are unengaged with marketing materials. This means they neither open marketing emails nor click through to an organization's website.

Among those who did open email marketing messages, the report clearly showed that triggered emails were more effective at generating click throughs. Overall, click-through rates on triggered emails - emails that are sent automatically based on users' online and social networking activities - were 122 percent higher than for standard emails, something that marketers appear to be taking into account when kicking off their campaigns.

“While the average business-as-usual email volume decreased 6 percent in the second quarter of 2012, we’ve seen a 14 percent increase in triggered email volume,” said Linda Woolley, DMA’s acting president and CEO. “This illustrates that direct marketers are evolving their email contact strategies to meet the changing needs and preferences of their consumers and responding to specific actions and behaviors.”

Advantages

There are a variety of benefits to using triggered emails, according to a recent article from Business2Community. By sending marketing material based on users' actions, an organization can be timely and relevant, engaging with consumers at exactly the moment they're most receptive to a particular message. And because users are more likely to engage with these types of emails, organizations can glean significantly more data, helping them further shape their campaigns.

Segmenting for best results

Organizations that utilize triggered emails often engage in segment-based personalization of their email lists. A recent survey of marketers from B2B Marketing and Circle Research showed that 39 percent of the respondents felt segmenting their email lists helps focus their email messages and generate leads, particularly in the B2B world, Business2Community recently reported.

Creating a marketing persona for email subscribers involves grouping individuals by certain characteristics using information collected about their computer and technology habits, and is one of the more effective ways to segment a list, suggests marketing expert Heidi Cohen. When taking this approach, there are several aspects of user personas to keep in mind, including:

  • Demographics - age, location, region, education
  • Lifestyle - income, spending habits
  • Interests - hobbies, religion, travel habits
  • Technology use - what devices are used to view information, overall use habits

Marketers can go even further when personalizing list segments by taking into account users' emotional perspectives, past behaviors and informational needs, Cohen said.

By properly managing their email marketing lists, marketers can take full advantage of triggered emails, a trend that has proven effective in engaging consumers, generating leads and increasing click through rates.
