IT pros, marketers should collaborate for optimal SEO

Improving an organization's search engine optimization (SEO) practices can often be a challenging process, in which actually implementing necessary changes in a content management system is only a minor step. Instead, marketers may find themselves spending time navigating departmental politics to institute new efforts. To secure help from the IT department, Search Engine Land columnist Ian Lurie wrote, SEO marketing professionals should work collaboratively with their counterparts on the tech side.

Lurie noted that both SEO and IT are often seen as cost centers rather than value generators. He suggested playing to this misconception by offering IT departments a way to demonstrate their value. If marketers can come to IT staff with data that shows the value of organic search in generating revenue, IT executives will be more inclined to help with marketing initiatives because doing so will provide them with revenue-generating metrics they can show to their own superiors.

Marketers can also gather more IT support by slowly building trust, Lurie said. He suggested beginning with a simple IT request that will boost SEO - a title tag change, for instance - and then tracking the effect of that change very closely. Each small success SEO staff can demonstrate to IT will increase IT's willingness to contribute to SEO efforts.

Considerations for collaboration

One reason that IT staff and marketers often fail to communicate successfully is that marketers tend to value creativity, while IT staff place more emphasis on data-driven analysis, strategy consultant Peter Kim told GigaOM. He agreed with Lurie that the marketing industry needs to have metrics it can show executives that will justify technology investments or social media initiatives.

Many marketers also have an aversion to big data, which is seen as a boring, back-end process, Kim said. IT and marketing staff can come together to improve understanding of data, ideally driving more aligned decision-making. Another important consideration for alignment is understanding budget cycles, according to Kim. While marketing budgets are generally handled on a quarterly basis, IT budgets often follow a three- or five-year cycle. These cycles need to be more closely aligned if the two departments are going to work together to implement new technology such as cloud software that will improve marketing effort.
